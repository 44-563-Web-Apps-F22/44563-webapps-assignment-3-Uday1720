Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya

The Anwar-Suhayli, which was created in Mughal emperor Akbar's studio, and a Mewar manuscript of the Hindu epic Ramayana from the 17th century are two notable manuscripts kept at the museum. An archaeological area was created with priceless sculptures and epigrams thanks to the transfer of coins and sculptures from the Poona Museum in Pune and the Royal Asiatic Society's Bombay branch. The British Museum's director, Neil MacGregor, gave an illustrated lecture on "World Cultures" in the museum's main foyer before opening the gallery.
